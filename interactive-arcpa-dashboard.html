Compliance Dashboard

An overview of key compliance risks and strategic recommendations for your PA program.

Demonstrable Linkage

The ARC-PA 5th Edition places heightened emphasis on the documented link between competencies, objectives, and assessments. A systematic approach is no longer optional.

Assessment Alignment Risk

Failures in assessment alignment (Standard B4.01a) are a leading cause of citations. This signals a critical need to validate that you are testing what you teach.

Actionable Recommendation

Mandate a program-wide curriculum audit using mapping matrices to visualize and verify alignment between objectives and assessments.

This chart highlights the most frequently cited standards, emphasizing the critical importance of assessment. The data clearly shows that Standard B4.01a, concerning the alignment of assessments with learning objectives, is a major area of concern for programs nationwide.

Compliance Risk Matrix

Use this matrix to self-assess your program's vulnerability. The goal is to move from high-risk, ad-hoc practices to low-risk, systematic processes that institutionalize compliance and quality.

ARC-PA Standard High Risk / No Formal Process Moderate Risk / Ad-Hoc Process Low Risk / Systematic Process
B4.01a: Assessment Alignment Assessments created by faculty with no formal review. Over-reliance on MCQs. Alignment discussed informally but not documented. Inconsistent across courses. Formal curriculum map links every objective to an assessment. Committee reviews for alignment.
B4.01b: Identifying Deficiencies Failures recorded but no standardized remediation process beyond re-testing. Remediation plans are inconsistent and faculty-dependent. Sporadic documentation. Formal, documented remediation policy. Data used to identify at-risk students early.
B1.01b: Competency Integration Competencies exist but are not explicitly linked to course objectives or assessments. Syllabi may list competencies, but the connection isn't mapped or tracked program-wide. Curriculum map explicitly links all course outcomes to program competencies.
C1.02: Continuous Improvement Data used for grading only. "Self-study" is a pre-accreditation activity. Individual course directors make changes, but no program-level review of data. Curriculum committee conducts annual review of aggregated data and documents action plans.

Core Educational Frameworks

Understanding the ARC-PA's educational architecture is fundamental to compliance. This section visualizes the key hierarchies and frameworks that govern curriculum design and assessment.

The ARC-PA Educational Hierarchy

This hierarchy shows how broad program goals are broken down into the specific, measurable actions that guide daily instruction and assessment. Hover over each level to see its official definition. A compliant program must demonstrate clear, documented links between all three levels.

Program Competencies

Broad, summative statements defining the abilities required for entry-level PA practice (e.g., Medical Knowledge, Patient Care).

Course Learning Outcomes

The measurable abilities attained by a student at the completion of a specific course or curricular component. They provide evidence of progress toward competencies.

Instructional Objectives

The most granular, specific, and measurable statements that guide student acquisition of required competencies within a single lesson or activity.
